Jordan Elias is a board-certified music therapist and counselor integrating talk therapy with creative projects to explore emotions, build resilience, and foster meaningful change. Services include individualized therapy sessions, community arts programming for youth centers — including bands, lessons, and performances — and creative arts therapy programs for outpatient cancer treatment at a hospital.

Therapeutic Approach

Jordan’s approach is collaborative and creative, rooted in understanding how past experiences shape present behaviors and beliefs. Therapy creates a safe environment to explore thoughts and emotions while incorporating creative outlets — music-making, art, and lyric analysis — to express feelings that may be difficult to articulate.

The goal is to empower clients to rewrite narratives that no longer serve them and build a sense of self they can be proud of.

Education & Credentials

Jordan holds a master’s degree in cognitive behavioral science from Freie Universität Berlin and an undergraduate degree in psychology and music therapy from the Berklee College of Music in Boston.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who does Jordan work with? Adults, neurodivergent individuals (autism, ADHD), musicians, and youth in community programs or hospitals.
  • What types of therapy are offered? Individual sessions, community music programs, creative arts therapy for cancer patients, and music-based lessons for youth.
  • Is prior musical experience required? No. Sessions are tailored to each individual's interests and abilities.
  • Can I participate online? Yes, individual therapy sessions are available online for remote clients.
