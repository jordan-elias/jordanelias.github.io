Jordan Elias is a board-certified music therapist and counselor integrating talk therapy with creative projects to explore emotions, build resilience, and foster meaningful change. Services include individualized therapy sessions, community arts programming for youth centers — including bands, lessons, and performances — and creative arts therapy programs for outpatient cancer treatment in hospitals.

Therapeutic Approach

Jordan’s approach is collaborative and creative, rooted in understanding how past experiences shape present behaviors and beliefs. Therapy creates a safe environment to explore thoughts and emotions while incorporating creative outlets — music-making, art, and lyric analysis — to express feelings that may be difficult to articulate.

The goal is to empower clients to rewrite narratives that no longer serve them and build a sense of self they can be proud of.

Education & Credentials

Jordan holds a master’s degree in cognitive neuroscience from Freie Universität Berlin and an undergraduate degree in psychology and music therapy from the Berklee College of Music in Boston. He is board certified by the Certification Board for Music Therapy and has completed over 1000 hours of clinical supervision as part of his training.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who does Jordan work with? Neurodivergent individuals (autism, ADHD), musicians, and people experiencing burnout, depression, or anxiety.
  • What types of therapy are offered? Individual sessions, community music programs, creative arts therapy for cancer patients, and adapted music lessons.
  • Is prior musical experience required? No. Sessions are tailored to each individual's interests and abilities.
  • Can I participate online? Yes, individual therapy sessions are available online for remote clients.
